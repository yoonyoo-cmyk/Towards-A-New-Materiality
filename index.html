01
Humans feed on information. It's how we understand, feel, and survive.But when we're surrounded by plain, shiny, flawless surfaces, there's nothing left for the body to read – no texture, no signal, no story. And so, without even knowing why, we begin to feel stressed.
Still, we've come to believe this is ideal. And when that ideal breaks – when things crack, fade, or show the trace of time – we throw them away.
In doing so, we lose more than just objects. We lose stories. Memories. Culture. And we leave behind waste – hurting both people and the planet.


02
Plastic made things lighter, faster, easier. But we’ve learned a lot since then. It’s made from finite resources, yet treated like it’s endless. It doesn’t disappear - it only breaks apart into microplastics. Now they’re everywhere: deep in the ocean, high on Everest, even inside us. This fuels overconsumption and unsustainable practices. 


03
In response to this, let’s shift our value on materiality, embracing its ‘non-ideal’ states.
